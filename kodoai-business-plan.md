# KodoAI Business Plan and Strategy

**Owner:** Soban Ahmad Malik (brand: KodoAI)
**Prepared:** June 2026. Flagship offer repositioned to Revenue and Ad Operations Agents.
**Stage:** Pre-revenue ($0 MRR). Target: $5,000 MRR.

Strategic source of truth behind the LinkedIn content engine, cold outreach, and every proposal. See `rules/operating-rules.md` for writing and research rules; this document does not repeat them.

---

## 1. Offers

Audit-first custom AI agent systems for paid-ads agencies and high-ticket service businesses: find the single manual process costing the most revenue or time, build the agent that removes it. Governing principle: if it is manual and its output is measurable, it can be automated.

**Flagship: Revenue and Ad Operations Agents.** An autonomous ad-ops agent that generates, deploys, A/B tests, and optimises creative across Meta, Google, and TikTok; monitors CTR, CPR, and ROAS in real time; catches creative fatigue and CPA spikes; reallocates budget automatically. Paired with a competitor ad-intelligence agent watching rival ad libraries for creative and spend shifts. Flagship for three reasons: it attacks the agency's single most expensive manual workload, it protects the relationship that keeps the agency alive (missed CPA spikes drive churn), and because it runs continuously it converts naturally to a monthly retainer, which is what the MRR goal needs.

**Pre-Sales Research Agent (demo and entry wedge).** Prospect URL in, branded pre-call brief in the AE's inbox in 60 to 180 seconds: company snapshot, marketing posture, evidenced pain points, conversation openers, pricing anchors, recommended lead service. The most demonstrable live asset and easiest "try it" hook, so it stays central to outreach even though ad ops is now the flagship revenue product.

**Client lifecycle automation.** Onboarding intelligence engines (kickoff transcript plus client URL produces a strategy brief before the call ends), intake portals (the AR&CO build), 24/7 after-hours intake and voice agents, and reporting agents pulling cross-platform data into client-ready reports. Reporting agents are a retention product: 73% of clients who fire an agency cite poor communication and no proactive updates (HubSpot, 2025).

**Content and outreach agents.** Video and ad-creative generation with human approval (the AR&CO build), and lead-generation agents that scrape and enrich prospect lists on a schedule.

Shape of the offer: lead with ad ops, open with a free Pre-Sales brief or ad-account teardown, expand into reporting, onboarding, intake, and content agents once inside the account.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile

**Primary: US paid-ads and performance marketing agencies**, 15 to 50 staff, $1.5M to $5M revenue (working hypothesis inferred from fee maths, not directly measured, keep testing against actual replies), founder/CEO/managing partner as sole buyer.

The ad-ops pain is large and worsening: 87% of agencies still pace budgets manually, 71% of ad-ops teams say manual processes put client campaigns at risk (Fluency 2026 AdOps Benchmark, 170-plus US agencies), 8 to 12 hours/week lost to manual bid and budget adjustment on $500k-plus monthly-spend accounts. Overspending an intended budget by more than 20% drops ROAS 15 to 25%; Meta creative fatigues within 7 to 14 days; strong versus average creative swings ROAS 2 to 3 times on identical targeting.

Economics: average digital agency runs roughly 13% net margin, and growth typically requires headcount that erodes it. An ad-ops agent lets a strategist cover more accounts without the manual hours scaling linearly. PPC and performance agencies churn at roughly 49% annually (Focus Digital, January 2026), versus about 18% for retainer shops; missed performance and opaque communication are the two biggest drivers, exactly what ad-ops monitoring and automated reporting fix.

Macro trigger: 22% of CMOs told Gartner AI reduced their need for external agencies; 60% of US senior marketing leaders spent less on agencies in 2025 because of AI; Forrester forecasts a 15% cut in agency jobs in 2026. Agencies that automate to defend margin and results survive this.

**Buying triggers:** a client churned or nearly did after an unnoticed CPA spike; strategists drowning in manual pacing across accounts; trying to add clients without adding headcount; creative fatiguing faster than the team refreshes it; lost a renewal over flat results or opaque reporting; managing 4-plus ad platforms manually (68% of agencies do); actively hiring media buyers or AEs.

**Secondary: US med spas (expanding).** Owner-operated, decides fast. Roughly 52,800 US med spas (about 60% single-owner), an $8.4bn 2025 market growing about 14%/year, roughly 35% of prospective clients try booking outside business hours, average visit worth $500 to $700. Fits after-hours intake/voice agents and ad-creative agents on their own paid social. Second beachhead once the agency motion is producing.

## 3. Value Proposition

For agencies: an autonomous ad-ops agent watching every client account 24/7, catching CPA spikes and creative fatigue before they cost the client, reallocating budget automatically, so strategists stop losing 8 to 12 hours a week to manual pacing and the agency can take on more accounts without adding headcount.

Deeper promise: KodoAI removes the mechanical 90% of any process with a measurable output, so the team spends hours on strategy that compounds revenue. In an ad account, manual work is not slow, it is money leaking in real time.

Credibility comes from named proof, not adjectives: a real ad-optimisation agent with fatigue detection and budget reallocation, a real branded brief, a real portal, all tied to AR&CO (live at arandcolaw.com, five-star Upwork review).

## 4. Pricing

Priced at or slightly below market to build reputation and case studies pre-revenue. Market context: AI automation builds run $2,500 to $15,000-plus, monitoring retainers $500 to $5,000-plus/month; comparable ad-ops point tools run $79/month (Madgicx) to $99 (Revealbot) to custom enterprise (Smartly), with some (Ryze) charging a percentage of spend.

| Component | Price |
|---|---|
| Ad-ops agent build (one-time) | $2,750 to $5,000 |
| Multi-agent system (one-time) | $6,500-plus |
| Other single-agent build (one-time) | $2,750 to $5,000 |
| Ad-ops monitoring retainer | $500 to $800/month |
| API and third-party costs | Passed through at zero markup |

The flagship is inherently continuous, so it converts to a retainer far more naturally than a one-time pre-sales build, shortening the path to $5,000 MRR. Future lever once results are proven: light percentage-of-spend pricing on the retainer. Always respect a stated client budget and position at or just below it; reputation matters more than a few hundred dollars right now.

## 5. Business Model

Productised-bespoke B2B services, solo operator (partner split on the brand). Two revenue streams: one-time builds (cash and proof) and monthly retainers (compounding MRR); ad ops as flagship tilts the balance toward recurring revenue. Delivery model: audit (increasingly a free ad-account teardown) then build then retain. Each engagement deepens account knowledge, making the next build inside that account easier to sell. Margins are structurally healthy since the main input is operator time plus zero-markup API pass-through.

## 6. Competitive Advantage

**Real, working builds, not promises.** A live Pre-Sales Agent producing a branded brief in under 90 seconds, and a built ad-generation-and-optimisation agent for AR&CO. The AI-automation-agency market grew from roughly 2,000 in 2024 to 12,000-plus in 2026, with an estimated 60% of 2025 launches completing fewer than five projects, so a visibly working build stands out immediately.

**Bespoke and integrated, versus point tools** (Madgicx, Revealbot, Smartly, AdCreative.ai). KodoAI builds into the agency's exact workflow, branding, and stack, connects it to reporting/intake/onboarding, passes API cost through at zero markup.

**Full-stack engineering depth.** Production systems (Next.js, NestJS, FastAPI, AWS, multi-agent pipelines with graceful degradation, Row-Level Security), not Zapier-and-ChatGPT glue. AR&CO (20-plus database tables, four user roles, delivered in 12 weeks) is the proof.

**ICP-specific positioning.** Speaks the agency's exact language: CPA spikes, budget pacing, creative fatigue, ROAS decay, tab-switching across platforms.

**Cost structure.** A Pakistan-based solo operator with zero-markup API pass-through delivers senior-grade, integrated systems at the accessible end of the price band, profitably.

Honest counterweight: the ad-ops space has more capable point-tool competition than the pre-sales niche did, so KodoAI must win on integration, fit, and trust rather than being first to a feature. The durable moat is the relationship, accumulated account knowledge, and a consistent public presence, which is what the LinkedIn and outreach engine builds.

## 7. Short-Term Goal

$0 to $5,000 MRR. At the top of the retainer band ($800/month), roughly six to seven ad-ops retainers reach the target; blended with builds and smaller retainers, the path is shorter than under a one-time-build flagship.

Milestone ladder: (1) first free ad-account teardown converts to a first paid ad-ops build, a second named case study beyond AR&CO; (2) that build goes onto a monitoring retainer; (3) three to four ad-ops engagements live, two to three on retainer (roughly $1,500 to $2,400 MRR); (4) six to seven ad-ops retainers, or an equivalent build-plus-retainer blend, hits the target.

Paid advertising is gated behind $5,000 MRR. Until then, growth runs entirely on owned and earned channels: LinkedIn, cold calling, cold email.

## 8. The 90-Day Plan

**The offer:** an autonomous ad-ops agent monitoring client accounts 24/7, catching CPA spikes and creative fatigue before they cost the client, reallocating budget automatically, so strategists stop burning 8 to 12 hours a week on manual pacing and the agency can take on more accounts without hiring.

**Door-opener:** a free ad-account teardown (read access or a few screenshots, in exchange for a specific breakdown of leaking spend and fatigued creative). Maps directly to the flagship and turns first contact into an evidenced conversation. The free Pre-Sales brief remains a secondary hook for agencies whose sharper pain is new-business rather than delivery.

**Sequence:** Months 1 to 2, establish credibility: LinkedIn content runs TOFU-heavy on ad-ops pain, cold outreach opens conversations (not pitches) offering the free teardown, goal is the first one or two paid ad-ops builds. Months 2 to 3, convert and stack: builds go onto retainers, MOFU/BOFU content shows real agent outputs, first warm replies from the connection campaign appear. Month 3 onward: case studies feed content, content warms outreach, outreach feeds builds, builds feed retainers.

**Patience rule:** with cold-called or messaged founders, the LinkedIn profile is the trust check. The content and connection campaign exist so that by the time KodoAI reaches out, the founder has already seen eight to ten specific, credible posts. Others pitch in 48 hours; KodoAI waits 60 days and converts far better for it.

## 9. SWOT

**Strengths:** flagship attacks the most expensive, most measurable daily pain and is recurring by nature; real working builds plus a shipped, reviewed platform; senior engineering depth; low cost base with zero-markup API pricing; sharp ICP positioning with a documented content and outreach system.

**Weaknesses:** $0 MRR, one fully external named case study, the ad-ops agent's results are still proposal-stage rather than a public client outcome; solo operator, so retainers consume operator time and create capacity risk; ad ops is trust-heavy and money-in-motion, a higher bar than a research brief; current LinkedIn audience is mostly Pakistani tech peers, not US agency founders.

**Opportunities:** the pain is quantified and worsening (figures above); the margin ceiling makes an ad-ops agent a direct enabler of more clients without more headcount; the retention crisis (49% churn, 73% citing poor communication) is fixable by the flagship plus a reporting agent; the in-house-AI shift makes automation-led defence urgent; the AI-automation market is flooded but shallow, leaving room for a visibly competent operator; a large, fast-growing secondary ICP in med spas.

**Threats:** a crowded ad-ops tool market (Madgicx, Revealbot, Smartly, AdCreative.ai, Ryze) that is cheaper, instant, and well-known; platform and policy risk on Meta/Google/TikTok APIs (mitigated by graceful degradation and human approval); reputational risk if an agent mismanages spend, making the human-approval layer a selling point rather than an afterthought; sophisticated agencies building internal ad-ops automation instead of buying; commoditisation as the AI-automation-agency count keeps climbing.

## 12. Competitors

Five tiers. **Ad-ops point SaaS** (Madgicx ~$79/mo, Revealbot ~$99/mo, Smartly enterprise, AdCreative.ai ~$39 to $49/mo, percentage-of-spend players like Ryze): real substitutes for individual features; KodoAI's edge is a bespoke agent integrated into the agency's exact workflow at zero-markup API cost, versus a generic product the agency must adapt to. **Generalist AI-automation agencies** (12,000-plus, most shallow): KodoAI beats them on demonstrability and ICP specificity. **No-code/workflow shops** (Zapier, Make, n8n): cheaper and faster but brittle and unfit for production-grade ad ops; KodoAI wins on engineering depth and reliability. **Other freelancers and offshore developers:** compete on price, rarely combine senior delivery with ICP-specific positioning and a public credibility engine. **The non-obvious competitor: the agency's own in-house team**, as AI lowers the bar to wiring up rules or a script themselves; the counter is integration depth, accumulated account knowledge, the human-approval safety layer, and a price that beats the opportunity cost of build-and-maintain.

## 13. Channels

Three owned/earned channels until $5,000 MRR; paid advertising is gated behind that milestone.

**LinkedIn (trust engine, not yet a lead channel):** TOFU-heavy calendar (four posts/week) weighted toward ad-ops pain, the connection campaign (10 to 20 manual requests/day to US paid-ads agency founders), 100-plus genuine comments/week on ICP creators. Low engagement from the current audience reflects the audience being rebuilt, not weak content.

**Cold calling:** direct to agency founders and med spa owners who answer their own phones. Opener is the free ad-account teardown, not a pitch.

**Cold email:** volume channel for the agency ICP, highly personalised, P&L-framed, always revenue/margin/competitive framing rather than time-saving framing, per the writing rules.

The three channels compound: outreach creates the touch, LinkedIn provides the trust check, the free teardown provides the proof, the 60-day rule lets warmth build before any ask.

## 14. Content and Outreach Execution

**Content by funnel stage:** TOFU (3/week) names the ad-ops pain precisely with no product, ends on a question or provocation. MOFU (1 to 2/week) shows real output (fatigue catches, budget reallocation, before/after ROAS), ends on a low-friction invite. BOFU (1/week or fortnight) proves it with the AR&CO case study and a direct teardown offer, ends on a one-step CTA. Every post: hook in line one, numbers where possible, short paragraphs, 3 to 5 hashtags, and the writing rules in `rules/operating-rules.md`.

**Content pillars:** ad operations as a margin and retention problem; build in public; manual work as a competitive liability.

**Lead magnet:** "The Ad-Account Leak Audit: 10 signals your client accounts are quietly losing money (most agencies miss 6 of them)," one page, distributed via "comment AUDIT and I'll send it over." The original Pre-Call Research Checklist remains a secondary magnet for new-business-focused prospects.

**Outreach mechanics:** prospect list of 100 to 250 US paid-ads agency founders via LinkedIn search (Advertising Services industry filter, owner/partner seniority), manual only. Connection requests 10 to 20/day, blank by default, personalised notes only for the top 30 priority targets/month. Intent-based DMs (post like, comment, connect) convert far better than cold; the 60-day rule applies throughout. Cold call and cold email both open with a specific observation about the prospect's ads and the free-teardown offer.

**The single most important sentence in the go-to-market:** "Give me read access or a few screenshots of one client ad account and I'll show you where it's leaking spend and which creative has fatigued, free."
